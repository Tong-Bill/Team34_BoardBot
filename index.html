Team 34â€™s goal is to create an intelligent, engaging, and responsive robotic Monopoly player. This product's target audience is directed towards those who desire a companion when playing board games, like the elderly and children. While robots capable of playing more traditional board games such as chess already exist, Boardbot explores the field of social interaction, a field widely left out in the current generation of robots. The main purpose of the project is to delve into human-robot interactions through the usage of robots and Monopoly in hopes of solving loneliness and other social issues prevalent in society.

Social Interaction

The robot interacts with people through the usage of face emojis, an announcer voice, and a Trump text-to-speech voice over trained from the Tortoise TTS machine learning model.

Computer Vision

Recognizing dice rolls with precision and separating Monopoly related objects on the boardspace from unrelated debris allows for semi-autonomous rounds to be performed by the robot.

Motion Planning

Using Rviz and the Gazebo simulator, arm & gripper actions from the Baxter robot can be planned out before physical execution to ensure the safety of users.

Game Rules

Correct Monopoly game mechanics and rules for operation are implemented to ensure fair play and familiarity for those who have played the official board game.

AI

Acting as the brain of the entire system, the AI is responsible for keeping track of the game state as well as receiving, processing, and sending information as needed to other components during a game session.
